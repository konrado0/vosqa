Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How is a Question/Answer community different from a typical forum?

A Question/Answer community is different from a typical forum because it focuses on finding answers to the questions of its members. On a typical forum, by contrast, discussion flows freely, rarely stays on topic and it is much harder to spot valuable answers.

By keeping a laser focus on questions and answers, this community ensures that finding answers to existing questions - and getting answers to new questions - can be done without any distractions.

What kinds of questions can I ask here?

Most importantly - questions should be relevant to {{settings.APP_TITLE}} community. Before you ask, please make sure to search for a similar question. You can search for questions by their title, content, or tags.

What kinds of questions should be avoided?

Please avoid purely argumentative questions.

What should I avoid in my answers?

{{settings.APP_TITLE}} is a question and answer site - it is not a discussion group. Please avoid holding debates in your answers as they tend to dilute the essense. For brief discussions please use comments.

Who moderates this community?

The short answer is: you. This website is moderated by the users. The community features a reputation system that allows users to earn rights to perform a variety of moderation tasks.

How does the reputation system work?

When a question or answer is upvoted, the user who posted it will be awarded "reputation points". These points serve as a rough measure of the community trust in this user. Various moderation tasks are gradually assigned to the users based reputation points.

For example, if you ask an interesting question or give a helpful answer, your input will be upvoted. On the other hand if the answer is misleading, it will be downvoted. Each vote in favor will generate {{ settings.REP_GAIN_BY_UPVOTED }} points, each vote against will subtract {{ settings.REP_LOST_BY_DOWNVOTED }} points. There is a limit of 200 points that can be accumulated per question or answer. The table below explains reputation requirements for each type of moderation task.

Delete comments{{ settings.REP_TO_DELETE_COMMENTS }}
Close own questions{{ settings.REP_TO_CLOSE_OWN }}
Reopen own questions{{ settings.REP_TO_REOPEN_OWN }}
Retag questions{{ settings.REP_TO_RETAG }}
Edit any answer{{ settings.REP_TO_EDIT_OTHERS }}
Open any closed question{{ settings.REP_TO_CLOSE_OTHERS }}
Delete any comment{{ settings.REP_TO_DELETE_COMMENTS }}

What is a gravatar?

Gravatar means globally recognized avatar - your unique avatar image associated with your email address. It's simply a picture that shows next to your posts on the websites that support gravatar protocol. The default gravatar appears as a square filled with a snowflake-like figure. You can set your image at gravatar.com or simply set your avatar in user settings.

Why can other people can edit my questions/answers?

Allowing experienced members of this community to curate the questions and answers improves the overall quality of the knowledge base content.
